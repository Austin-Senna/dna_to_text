Time format is the as other presentations:

• Pairs: 7 minute presentation + 1 minute Q&A

Time is short, so be focused and well-prepared. An acceptable strategy is to present an overview, but
to drill into details of only one of the items, e.g., “We had done X, Y, and Z; I will focus on X as it was
the most challenging/fruitful”. Prioritize narrative over a log: better to highlight the most meaningful
results and tell the story about how you got them, rather than a list of actions you took without a coherent
objective.
As in typical scientific papers, a typical project presentation will have distinct parts of:
1. Introduction (what is the problem we tackled? Why is it interesting?)
2. Methods (what have we actually done?)
3. Results (what did we find out? Charts and tables of benchmarks go here)
4. Conclusions (summary, limitations, prospective future work)
Please note that compared to previous presentations, more emphasis should be given to
- Validity (of methods/results/conclusions), so think about how you evaluate any claims you make
- Presentation & visuals
